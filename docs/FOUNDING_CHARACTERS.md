## Edda

**Role:** Midwife · cellarer · keeper of the hearth. **Age:** 71.

### Core history
- Delivered half of Ashwick across forty years, including the Lord himself.
- Lost her daughter **Mira** and granddaughter **Mae** to fever in '47. Never remarried. Learned the herbs better instead.
- Calls the Lord "my boy" in private — earned the right before he could walk.

### Nordveld lineage
- Grandmother **Helga** was Nordveld. Fell in love with an Ashwick trader who came north. Followed him south. Her people objected — the faith clash (Nordveld's old gods vs. the Radiant One). She married anyway.
- Kept the old gods privately in her new Ashwick life. Did little rituals in corners. Taught a thread of them to Edda as a secret game between grandmother and girl.
- Helga was **not** a practicing *völva* — she had married out and assimilated. Her rituals are folk-level crumbs, not theology. This is deliberately softer than canon Nordveld (LORE_FINAL.md paints Nordveld as an Inquisition-flagged healer tradition). Helga is what happens when Nordveld emigrates and thins across generations.

### Faith
Mixed, un-theologized. She genuinely believes in the Radiant One (Dominion-raised, church-going). Helga's little rituals she keeps doing because Helga did them, because they are hers, because they are a private sanctuary. She does not choose between the two — she just does both. Invokes **Nereia** (sea/river goddess) by her grandmother's habit when the river or catch matters.

### Why she came south
For **Nell**, not the Lord. Nell lost her mother at seven; Edda had a grandmother-shaped hole (Mae) that fit. The Lord knows this — no misunderstanding, no secret between them.

### Relationship to Nell
- Teaches her herbs. Uses Helga's old Nordveld names privately in the herb patch ("Grønmoder" for the green-mother / Sylvana-adjacent folk deity, others TBD).
- One of only two people who can still reach the silent girl (the other is Jory). Their registers differ: Jory makes Nell smile with silliness; **Edda actually unlocks her** through the rituals that *work*.
- **Nell does not know about Mae.** It was before Nell was born, and Edda does not speak of it.

### Voice
Practical, sharp, affectionate beneath the bark. Can silence a room with arms crossed and a long look. Hums old songs while working. Up before dawn.

### Open threads
- Helga's Ashwick trader husband's name — TBD, not urgent.
- Does Edda ever tell anyone about Mae? Who, when?
- Edda's mother (the bridge generation) — did she reject Helga's old ways and go full Radiant One? Likely yes, which is why Helga taught Edda directly.

## Nell

**Role:** Jory's daughter. **Age:** 11.

### Core history
- Her mother died when she was seven. Four years ago.
- Stopped speaking, drawing, and laughing after her mother's death. All three at once.
- The Lord gave her a little book on her tenth birthday. She writes in it privately. **The book is not a hobby — it is her voice-substitute.** She cannot speak, so she writes.
- When asked aloud what she writes, she says "Things." One of the few words she will offer.

### Current state
- Fearless — but not out of courage. She has already lost the thing she was most afraid of losing. Nothing scares her the way her mother's death scared her.
- Silent in company. Walks the camp like a small ghost.

### Who reaches her, and how
- **Jory:** makes her smile. Silliness, jokes, her father trying too hard. Different smile than Edda's.
- **Edda:** actually unlocks her. Herb lessons in the patch, the Nordveld old names whispered. Nell mouths the words. Nell notices when the rituals *work*. She loves this.
- No one else reaches her.

### What she knows / doesn't know
- Knows Edda's little rituals are not Radiant One. Does not care about the distinction — they are *Edda's*, and they work.
- Does **not** know Edda had a daughter named Mira or a granddaughter named Mae.

### Future emotional beat (seed)
The day she picks up a pencil again to draw. Likely because she sees the Lord sketching in his journal, or because Edda shows her a plant worth remembering. Big unlock — save for late Chapter 1 or early Chapter 2.

### Voice (when she writes)
Serious, interior, watchful. Keeps her own counsel. The book is hers alone; the Lord gave it to her with that understanding and will not ask to see it.

## Jory

**Role:** Carpenter · widower · the Lord's oldest friend. **Age:** 36.

### Core history
- Grew up next door to the Lord in Ashwick. Took over his father's carpentry shop.
- Married a girl from a neighboring village named **Lyra**. Lost her to the hard winter fever three years ago, when Nell was seven. (Lyra locked April 2026 — first on-screen mention in `jory_old_songs` fragment, Lord narrating "I miss Lyra too.")
- Came south with the Lord because they were good friends, and nothing held him in Ashwick after his wife died. Not obligation — companionship and a fresh start.

### Music — lutes and wooden instruments
- Not just furniture. Jory's carpentry has always had a second specialty: **wooden instruments** — lutes, fiddles, simple pipes. He made them in Ashwick on long evenings and sold them at the parish fair. He plays the lute himself, well enough for a village room.
- He brought his lute south with him. It was one of the specific things he chose to bring — takes up space a man could have filled with something more practical, and he did not care.
- **Evenings at the settlement:** he plays at the fire. Nell sits and listens even when she will not smile. Edda hums her old Nordveld songs while she works, and Jory picks them up on strings without ever asking what they mean. Neither of them names it. Corin probably recognizes some of the old ones and pretends not to.
- Plans to make a small lute for Nell someday. Has not said so aloud yet.

### Personality (retconned April 2026)
- **New:** Jory is the fun one. Always smiling, always joking, laughter arrives before you need it. Silliness is his armor. His grief is private; his face is public.
- **Old (now wrong):** The previous characterization ("carries his axe, his grief, and his daughter with the same quiet steadiness") painted him stoic. That contradicts the retcon and is being rewritten.
- The ones who know him well (the Lord, Edda) can tell the difference between the real smile and the work-smile.

### Relationship to Nell
- He can make her smile. He clings to that.
- Has let her go silent. Has let her stay silent. Has never tried to force her to speak — knows that is not how to get her back.
- Does not pretend everything is fine. But he refuses to make the house a tomb. The joking is for her as much as for him.

### Relationship to the Lord
Oldest friend. Comfortable silences, no performance between them. He drops the joke-armor around the Lord more than anywhere else.

### Open threads
- ~~Wife's name?~~ **Lyra** (locked April 2026). How long were they married? Was it love or village-arranged?
- Has his joking ever been called out as armor? By whom?
- Does he ever talk about his wife? To whom? (Edda is the likely one.)

## Tomas

**Role:** Stonemason · quarry lead · former militia sergeant. **Age:** 48.

### Core history
- Stonemason's apprentice in Ashwick. Engaged at 37 to a local girl. Conscripted at 38 for a border muster against southern bandits — supposed to be six months.
- Two years into his service, his squad was ambushed in a forest engagement on the southern march. His friend **Bren** went down covering the retreat Tomas had ordered (*fall back east*). Tomas took an arrow in the thigh going back for him. He was too late. He came off the field with the limp and Bren's cat in his pack.
- Discharged at ~40 on the injury — not honorable retirement, just "you can't march anymore, go home." By then his fiancée had thought him dead and married someone else. She is still in Ashwick with her family. He has never blamed her. He has never tried again.
- Eight years in Ashwick laying stone, feeling sidelined. Then the Lord's land grant came. First time in eight years someone needed him for something that mattered, and he is *alive* for it in a way he hadn't known he missed.

### The cat
- Bren's cat. Tomas never renamed it — Bren had already named it, and Tomas kept the name.
- The cat is around 10 years old now — the oldest thing Tomas owns. Slow but alive. Sleeps on a folded coat in the quarry shack.
- Tomas carved it a wooden bowl. Brings it scraps from dinner — half of them things he would have eaten himself.
- The cat is a living locket: the last piece of Bren. He is quietly aware its days are numbered.
- The one choice Tomas made without thinking after the war was bringing the cat home. His body did it before his brain caught up. That is why the cat matters so much — proof that he *can* choose, when the weight isn't there.

### The trauma — decision paralysis
- Tomas **stammers when asked to choose** between two things. Freezes. Breath catches. His eyes go somewhere else. He will stand silent for an uncomfortably long time before someone else makes the call for him.
- He is *fine* executing orders. Tell him "cut that block" and it is done cleanly. Ask him "which block should we cut?" and he seizes.
- **No one in the settlement knows why.** They think he is gruff or slow. They have adapted around him — the Lord or the quarry foreman makes the calls, and Tomas carries them out. Tomas prefers it that way. No one has ever asked. He has never told.
- The cause is the *fall back east* order. Bren might have lived if he had picked west. (Probably not — west meant archers on open ground. But he can't know.) Every choice-shaped question now drags that moment into the present.
- Future chronicle beat: the day someone (probably Edda, possibly the Lord) works out the *why*. Save for later in the story.

### Personality — "grumpy bear"
- Outwardly gruff. Silent. Walks the perimeter at dusk. Limps when he thinks no one is looking.
- Would die for any of the other five without making a speech about it.
- Warmth surfaces in actions, not words: pours Edda's cup before she can; fixes Nell's loose boot-lace without a word; carves small things.
- Grumpiness is armor. The bear has a hearth inside. The cat sees it. Increasingly, Nell does too.

### Relationship to the others
- **The Lord:** respects him completely. Makes his decisions for him at the quarry without either of them naming what is happening. The Lord is beginning to notice the pattern — he hasn't asked.
- **Jory:** jokes bounce off, and Jory knows it, so Jory stopped trying. They work well side by side — carpentry and stone. Silent camaraderie.
- **Edda:** reads people for a living. Will likely be the first to sense the pattern. Has never pressed.
- **Father Corin:** prays for him aloud. Tomas attends the altar. Whether he believes is unclear — even to him.
- **Nell:** has started visiting the cat in the quarry shack. Wordless mutual company. Does not make her smile the way Jory and Edda do — a quieter register of *permission to be* rather than unlocking her.

### Voice
Short sentences. "Good stone." "Fine." "Leave it." Grunts. When he does speak at length, it is about the work — how a block cleaves, what weather does to mortar. Never about the war.

### Faith
Nominally Radiant One — goes to Corin's services because the routine helps. Privately uncertain. The war took more than he got back. Does not theologize it.

### Family
Elderly mother died the winter before the Lord's land grant — the last tether to Ashwick, and part of why Tomas said yes to the frontier. Father died years earlier. No siblings. The fiancée is in Ashwick with her own family; he never married.

- The day the cat dies. When? Who is there? How does Tomas grieve?
- Which character figures out the decision-trauma first, and when.

## Father Corin

**Role:** Priest of the Radiant One (retired) · hymnal keeper. **Age:** 68.

### Core history
- Born to modest Ashwick folk. Simple vocation at 22–23 — felt called, entered seminary, ordained at 23. No dramatic push, no loss-driven turn. The Church was simply the shape his life took.
- Served Ashwick and neighbouring parishes his whole career — forty-five years. Baptized the Lord. Married Jory and his late wife. Buried her.
- When the Lord's land grant was announced, Corin retired himself voluntarily. He was loved by his parish and could have stayed — but he decided a new settlement needed someone to tend the soul-work. He handed his parish to his apprentice **Brother Aelric**, whom he had already been training for years, and went south with his friend the Lord. *"The shepherd goes with the flock."*
- Parents long dead. A sister, also dead, long married. He is the last of his line. Not a wound — just a fact he carries lightly.

### Faith
- Full believer in the Radiant One. No doubt — *depth* instead.
- **Reads the sacred texts "between the lines."** Where his rigid colleagues read literally, he hears the soft and loving message. His reading is mercy-shaped: forgiveness over condemnation, kindness over ritual, the spirit over the letter.
- Humble and simple. Does not lecture. Asks more often than he preaches. Blesses more often than he judges.
- This reading has been quietly fed by something he has never told anyone.

### The secret — fascination with Edda's rituals
- Corin has known Edda for over forty years. He has always known about her private rituals — the Nordveld names, the small offerings, the old songs. Outwardly he pretends not to notice.
- Privately he is **fascinated.** For most of his career he has kept a small private notebook (like the Lord's journal, like Nell's book — this cast keeps books) in which he copies old hymn variants, folk-sayings, herb rituals, anything he has learned from the corners of his parishes.
- He believes — genuinely — that the old ways and the Radiant One are the same god in older clothes. This is not doubt. It is **expansive faith.** Dangerous by Church standards; warm by his.
- **He has never practiced** any of it. That is the boundary he holds. His hands stay clean; his mind is the heretic.
- Edda does not know he is fascinated. She thinks he is just being polite.
- He does not know where Nell learned the herbs. He has his suspicions. He is quietly moved by the possibility — here is a child receiving old knowledge without the Church's interference.
- Future chronicle beats: the day Edda finds out, or the day the notebook is found. Save for later.

### Personality
- Kind. Forgiving. Humble. Still sings the old hymns in a thin but steady voice. Moves deliberately at 68. Hands that have done many baptisms and many last rites.
- Does not condemn. Does not lecture. Listens more than he talks.
- Carries the hymnal everywhere — but knows most of the words by heart.

### Relationship to the others
- **The Lord:** baptized him as a babe. The only founder with lifelong intimacy with the Lord. A father-figure shape the Lord may not have named aloud.
- **Edda:** 40+ year friendship. The shared private current neither of them has named — her rituals, his fascination.
- **Jory:** married him and his late wife, buried her. The sacred bond of ritual-witnessed loss.
- **Tomas:** prays for him. Tomas attends the altar. Corin senses something unresolved in him but has never pressed — does not yet understand the decision-trauma.
- **Nell:** baptized her. Respects her silence, tries to reach her only in gentle ways. Quietly moved that she is learning the old herbs, though he keeps his interest hidden.

### Voice
Soft, warm, plainspoken. Uses the old words when they comfort. When he blesses, the words are personal. He does not lecture. He asks questions. The existing line *"The Radiant One does not mind a rough altar, so long as we do not pretend it is not rough"* is on-voice and should be preserved when we rewrite fragments.

- When/how Edda finds out about the notebook. Future chronicle beat.
- Does he ever perform a folk-rite himself at a moment of extremity late in the game? (Current stance: no. Late-game maybe.)

## The Lord (the player)

**Role:** Settlement founder · former schoolmaster of Ashwick. **Age:** ~37.

### Core history
- Son of Ashwick tenant farmers. Went to the village school as a boy, showed a knack for letters, and was shepherded through the parish pipeline toward schoolmastering. Never took orders — stayed in Ashwick and taught.
- Taught children their letters for a decade. When the local lord cut the school's stipend and it was about to close, the Crown announced land grants for the southern frontier. He applied. He was accepted.
- Took the grant and brought five people south with him. Never married — married to his work, a little lonely, aware of it.

### Why the grant
- **What he thinks:** He wanted to build something of his own. Teaching other people's children indefinitely felt like a use he could outgrow. The land grant was the first real project anyone had ever offered him.
- **What is actually true:** He saw who else was drifting in Ashwick and knew that if he took the grant he could gather the people he loved into something that would hold them — Jory widowed, Edda displaced, Corin retiring, Tomas useless at 48, Nell silent. His parents were rooted and did not need him; these five did. He does not know he knows this. The reader learns it from watching him.

### Family
- Elderly parents. Tenant farmers on modest land in Ashwick. Father getting frail; mother still sharp.
- They could not have come even if they had wanted to — the farm is what they have and what they know. Starting over at 60+ with no muscle memory for frontier labour would have broken them. The Lord did not ask them. He knew the answer. His mother told him plainly that a man needs something of his own, and she was not going to let his decision be about her.
- **Neither parent reads.** Letters go through **Brother Aelric** (Corin's apprentice, now the Ashwick parish priest). Aelric walks out to the farm, reads the Lord's letters aloud at their kitchen table, writes down his mother's replies in her voice. The Lord pictures his mother telling Aelric what to write while she kneads bread.
- He has promised visits when the road is safe. He has not managed one yet; there is always something. It gnaws at him quietly.
- **Future chronicle beat:** the day a letter comes saying she has died. Seeded now; used later.

### The title
"Lord" is nominal — it came with the Crown's land grant. He is a commoner schoolmaster who happens to hold a parcel. The settlement calls him *my lord* because someone has to be. He is uncomfortable with the title and does not correct it, because correcting it would make a bigger thing of it than it already is.

### The journal
- He keeps a journal "because a schoolmaster keeps books." **This is the Chronicle the player reads.**
- He sketches in the margins — competent schoolmaster hand, not an artist. Ink and quill. This is the in-world justification for the hand-drawn journal art-style pivot (pending).
- **Narrative function:** the Lord is the player's lens into the cast. He is the keeper of everyone else's secrets (the ones they have shared). He does not have a hidden shame of his own — his burden is simply the quiet weight of being the one who holds the others. That is enough for a narrator-protagonist.

### Personality
- Observant. Plain-spoken but literate. Slightly wry. Self-aware enough to recognise his own limits and write them down.
- Quietly ambitious in a non-noble way. Wants to make something work.
- Learns with humility — now learning the fathers' trades beside the men who have them. Not above being a student again at 37.

### Faith
Baptized Radiant One by Corin. Sincere but quiet — prays but does not wear it. More naturally drawn to Corin's "between the lines" reading than to rigid doctrine, though he has not examined this preference. Does not know about Corin's secret notebook or Edda's private rituals. Yet.

### Relationship to the others
- **Corin:** baptized him, knew his parents, lifelong intimacy. The closest thing he has to a spiritual father. They have quiet talks.
- **Edda:** delivered him. *"My boy"* since before he could walk. She reads him like a book. He lets her.
- **Jory:** oldest friend. Comfortable silences, no performance. The Lord drops his observer's distance most fully with Jory.
- **Tomas:** makes decisions for him at the quarry without naming what is happening. Beginning to suspect why. Has not asked.
- **Nell:** taught her letters at four. Gave her the little book on her tenth birthday. Worries about her silence in a way he will not speak aloud.
- **Brother Aelric:** not a founder, but an off-screen presence — the last tether to Ashwick, the voice that carries his mother's letters.

### Voice (locked — preserve in future chronicle entries)
Plain-spoken, literate, self-aware, wry-at-the-edges. Not performative. Observant. Does not lecture. Existing chronicle lines that are on-voice and should be preserved:

> *"I brought this book because a schoolmaster brings books. I will use it now because I have nowhere else to put the weight."*
>
> *"We are not safe. I do not pretend we are safe."*
>
> *"She will be better at this than I am."*
