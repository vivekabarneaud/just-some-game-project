# The Deep Seals — Lore Document

## Overview

Beneath the Ironspine Mountains, the Khazdurim mine system intersects with an ancient prison built by a pre-Sundering civilization. The prison holds the King of Demons — something older than the Sundering, older than the dormant gods, older than recorded history.

## The Ancient Seals

- Built by an unknown civilization before the Sundering
- Massive in scale — the original builders understood what they were containing
- Held perfectly for millennia until the Sundering cracked them
- The cracks are small relative to the Seals, but even a small crack in something this large is enough

## What Happened

The Sundering damaged the ancient Seals. Not enough to break them — but enough to let things through. Lesser demons slipped through the cracks and escaped into the Khazdurim tunnels and eventually into the wider world.

The Khazdurim understood immediately how bad the situation was. They didn't panic — they engineered. They added their own Seals on top of the ancient ones. Layers of rune-wards, reinforced stone, dwarven craftsmanship at its most desperate and determined.

## What's Behind the Seals

- **Lesser demons** already escaped through the cracks and roam the world, doing evil — it's their nature
- **Greater demons** are too large to fit through the cracks. Yet. They press and test and wait.
- **Bahruun, Warlord of the Third Depth** — the most powerful demon to breach the Seals so far. Broke through the second Seal and slaughtered an entire Anvil Guard team, then dismissed the two surviving sisters (Brunhild and Magna Hammerfall) as not worth killing. The Khazdurim know him by this title. What they don't know is his true title among demonkind: **Herald of the Deep** — because Bahruun isn't the endgame. He's the announcement that the King is coming.
- **The King of Demons** — something vast, ancient, and patient. The Khazdurim haven't seen it. They don't need to. They can feel it down there, scheming, waiting. It knows the Seals are weakening. The King uses manipulation as much as force — corrupting Anvil Guard elders, carving names in the Hall of Names to lure key dwarves away from their posts.

## The Khazdurim Response — The Watch

The vast majority of the Khazdurim stayed in the mountains. Their entire civilization reoriented around one purpose: reinforce the Seals and keep watch. Like a dwarven Night's Watch — a sworn duty to hold a line the rest of the world barely knows about.

- They don't talk about it with outsiders. Not out of shame — out of duty. The fewer people who know, the fewer who might do something foolish.
- The Watch is not glamorous. It's generations of dwarves maintaining locks on a door, knowing that if they stop, everything ends.
- "Ask a Khazdurim about the Deep Seals and watch how fast the conversation ends."

## Why Some Leave

Every Khazdurim adventurer is someone who broke rank from a civilization dedicated to holding the line. They fall into three types:

1. **The ones who fled** — They saw too much, went too close to the lower levels, or the weight of knowing broke them. They carry the knowledge like a wound.

2. **The hunters** — Demons escaped through the cracks and are loose in the world. Some Khazdurim leave the mountain specifically to track and kill them. They bring their skills to the surface not to abandon the Watch, but to extend it.

3. **The ones who cracked** — Not everyone can spend a lifetime listening to something terrible press against a door. Some simply needed to leave before the mountain swallowed them whole.

## Relationship to Other Lore

- **Not related to Netheron or the dormant gods.** The King of Demons predates all of it. This is a separate axis of evil — pure, ancient, unrelated to the theological struggles of the surface world.
- **Not related to the Sundering's cause.** The Sundering cracked the Seals as collateral damage, not because the demons caused it.
- **The rest of the world doesn't know.** Some demons are loose and people encounter them, but they don't know the source. The Khazdurim keep it that way on purpose.
